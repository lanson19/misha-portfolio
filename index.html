Home Arduino and Raspberry Pi Robot

I had originally started building this robot during the beginning years of high school. My goal was to build a personal robot with the physical capabilities to move around on its own and pick up objects, thus I went with a wheeled design and then a clawed arm on top for object manipulation. Initially I built it using random materials found around the house, focusing on resourcefulness and completely spontaneous prototyping.

I even prototyped a spatial awareness solution using an ultrasonic sensor mounted on a servo as radar. It worked decently, but when I completely redesigned and rebuilt the robot the summer after high school ended, I decided not to use it. To begin redesigning, I created a 3D CAD model in Autodesk Inventor.

This allowed me to position every component exactly where I wanted, and see how everything would fit together. It also allowed me to determine sizing for components I would manufacture such as the wooden base, metal brackets for motors, and the metal arm. I manufactured these parts at my school's metal shop, primarily using a mill and drill. The wooden base I cut out at my home using a hand saw. Holders for the battery and electronics I 3D printed.
